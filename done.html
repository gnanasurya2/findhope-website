I belong from a nuclear family and being a single child I have always been pampered by my parents since childhood. They have always kept me in a cocoon that assured confort level at its best. I have always had problems regarding anxiety and breathlessness but neither my parents nor me took them at a serious level.

In class 11, I came in a relationship that I can say was very close to my heart. I shifted to Bangalore for my undergraduate degree, away from my parents resulting in a long distance relationship as well. The big new city didn't really welcome me well lifestyles of people here are completely different. I started secluding myself from everything. Gradually I would see myself crying at the end of each day, not able to sleep at night due to regular panick attacks, sit quietly throughout the day in class, I even stopped communicating properly with my parents and had regular fights in my relationship.

I was assigned a therapist who diagnosis me with Anxiety Disorder, Major Depressive Disorder, Stress Disorder and a very low serotonin level in my body. I started taking antidepressants. Life wasn't easy still. Then I fractured my ankle. i was confined to bed for 45 days following which, I had to attend my end semester exams and complete an internship in that devastated state of mind. I was completely broken. Life felt meaningless. I started having rigorous panick attacks everyday and would break down in tears then and now.

Just before Diwali, I lost my grandmother who was extremely close to me. I started fearing death every sec and had several sleepless nights.But then I had my therapist, my best friend and my roommate who took care of me each and every second, I slowly shared everything with my parents and that was the best decision ever. It's due to the regular therapies, and constant support from everyone that 6 months have passed and I have been able to adjust with my college pretty well. Mental health is as normal as any other body disease. Antidepressants shouldn't be labelled as drugs. They are as common as paracetamol taken during fevers.
