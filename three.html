I lost my little brother to suicide on 6th January, 2019. He was only 18 years old. It was very sudden, shocking and happened when we least expected it. The days that followed were hard, it was the darkest phase of my life. There was trauma, there was grief, there was anger and I was an ugly mix of emotions. It's a feeling you just can't describe, I was full yet felt so empty. I remember praying, I'd go to bed hoping for a sign and then I just stopped sleeping altogether. I wouldn't sleep for weeks at a time and would cry uncontrollably.

There was this one time when I experienced a severe panic attack in the middle of the night, I couldn't move, I couldn't speak. I just wept, with my mum right next to me. I wouldn't wish this upon anyone. I eventually told my parents that it was too much and I wanted to get better. This is when my parents took me to a doctor and I was diagnosed with Anxiety, PTSD and insomnia. I've been on meds ever since and I can safely say that I'm in a much better place now.

I'm still learning to deal with my grief and coming to terms with my reality, a life without my brother but I am blessed with a very supportive and loving family and some amazing friends. They've helped me get better. Another thing that has helped is finding meaning in my loss. I am now running various campaigns and giving talks on mental health awareness and suicide prevention. It's the need of the hour. And to be completely honest, Raghav isn't truly gone. He's still here, guiding me from above. Giving me strength to do everything that I'm doing right now.
