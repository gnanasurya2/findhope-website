There was a time in my life when I had started to lose interest in most of the activities and things around me. Episodes of feeling lonely and disconnected were getting common. There would be times I would cry for no reason. I'd wake up with anxiety, and on some days I'd just lay in my bed tired but unable to sleep. I would constantly overthink and go in a downward spiral. This had reached a point where I decided to see a psychiatrist, the acceptance of the fact that there is something wrong with me and is beyond my ability to deal was hard at first but I had a friend who just stood by me like a pillar no matter what and honestly It's because of her I was able to see through this dark tunnel that my life was turning into and seek some light. So anyway, from there started my journey to fix myself.

I've gotten a lot better in dealing with my anxiety and have accepted it as a part of who I am. Every Once in a while I'll get thoughts that would make me really anxious, initially I used to give in to these thoughts and let them control me, but overtime I have managed to let go of these thoughts and just let them pass without any judgement. I think the solution for any mental health related issue comes from accepting it and talking about it, but we live in a society where it is still a taboo to talk about such issues. People are often afraid of being judged so they choose to suffer in silence. After realising how common this problem is, I decided to turn my struggle into art and inspire more and more people to look inwards and make sure that their mental health is not compromised. I started a podcast to help people get through their struggle and find the support that they might not be getting from family or friends.

The lows that I've been through during these past few months have made me a stronger person. It has led me on a journey of working on my own self that would never have happened if it wasn't for my beloved depression and anxiety.
